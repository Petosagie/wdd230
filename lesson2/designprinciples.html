Visual Hierarchy

Nike

Visual Hierarchy is the concept of using changes in font sizes to show a hierarichal level of information. Nike's website is an example of this design, it does a great job using visual hierarchy by displaying what is important right at the top where the viewer first sees the information. In this case it displays current sales and all information for the consumer at the top.

White Space

Google

The principle of Alignment is evident in this website. First, the navigation bar items have centered vertical alignment. Next, the header items have the same vertical alignment. Then, the title, description, and buttons in the body have centered horizontal alignment.
Go To Google

Hick's Law

Dstv

The DSTV website uses Hick's Law very well where it gives the user only but a few options so that they can make their decision allot quicker. It helps users simplify their decision-making process but at the same time not completely remove it. The U.S. Navy was quick to recognize the importance of the “Keep It Simple and Straightforward” (“K.I.S.S.”) principle, which reflects Hick’s Law.
